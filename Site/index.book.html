Featured Reviews

The Nightingale

Set in Nazi-occupied France during WWII, The Nightingale tells the story of two sisters—Vianne Mauriac and Isabelle Rossignol—who resist the German occupation in their own distinct ways. While Vianne struggles to protect her daughter and help Jewish children escape, Isabelle joins the French Resistance, becoming the celebrated "Nightingale" who guides downed Allied airmen across the Pyrenees to safety.

The Great Alone

A breathtaking story of survival and resilience set in the Alaskan wilderness. Kristin Hannah carefully crafts her works with the powerful narrative of love, loss, and the enduring strength of community. Read our in-depth review to discover why this book is a must-read.

Dear America, Notes of an Undocumented Citizen

In this powerful memoir, Pulitzer Prize-winning journalist Jose Antonio Vargas reveals his life as an undocumented immigrant in America. Read more about why Varga's story is a must read!
