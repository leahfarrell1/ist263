Non-Fiction

Evicted by Matthew Desmond

Genre: Nonfiction/Sociology ★★★½ (3.5/5 Stars)

Evicted follows eight families in Milwaukee struggling with housing insecurity. Desmond shows how eviction perpetuates poverty in America.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Strengths
  • Thorough Research
  • Compelling Personal Narratives
  • Strong policy analysis
Limitations
  • Repetitive at times
  • Occasional loss of narrative focus
  • Milwaukee-specific focus limits broader application
Themes
  • Housing Insecurity
  • Economic Struggles
  • The impact of poverty on individuals

Romance

The Wish by Nicholas Sparks

Genre: Contemporary Romance ★★★★★ (5/5 Stars)

A terminally ill photographer reflects on her first love on Ocracoke Island. Sparks blends past and present beautifully.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Why Five Stars?
  • Flawless narrative structure
  • Deep character authenticity
  • Meaningful exploration of photography as metaphor
  • Balance of romance and life reflection
Emotional Impact

The novel delivers an extraordinary emotional punch without feeling manipulative. Sparks handles terminal illness and first love with equal sensitivity, creating genuine moments that resonate long after the final page.

Themes
  • Young love
  • Family relationships

Part of Your World by Abby Jimenez

Genre: Contemporary Romance ★★★★½ (4.5/5 Stars)

Two people from different worlds—a big-city doctor and a small-town carpenter—form a deeply emotional connection.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Standout Elements
  • Sharp, witty dialogue
  • Complex family dynamics
  • Authentic exploration of class differences
  • Well-developed supporting characters
  • Perfect balance of humor and serious themes
Minor Drawbacks
  • Some plot resolutions feel slightly rushed
  • Secondary antagonist's arc could be more developed
Themes
  • Family obligation
  • Unlikely Romance
  • Domestic violence
